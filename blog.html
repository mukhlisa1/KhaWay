Welcome to KhaWay: Transforming English learning online

At KhaWay, we are redefining how children and teenagers learn English in a digital world. Our online English school combines innovative teaching methods, expert educators, and engaging content to create a unique learning experience tailored for young learners.

How many teachers and students are there in KhaWay?

Number of Teachers: Currently, Khaway School boasts a team of 35 skilled and certified teachers. Each teacher brings their unique expertise and enthusiasm to the classroom, ensuring every lesson is engaging and effective.

Number of Students: With a diverse student base of over 150 learners, we cater to children and teenagers from various countries, backgrounds, and skill levels.

Stories from teachers

Ms. Sofia – Turning Nervousness into Confidence

"One of my most memorable students was 10-year-old Alina. She was shy during her first class and struggled to speak even basic sentences. I designed games and storytelling exercises that allowed her to practice without feeling pressured. By the end of the term, Alina was confidently introducing herself and even telling short stories in English. Watching her grow has been one of my proudest moments as a teacher."

Mr. Jacob – Making Grammar Fun

"As a grammar enthusiast, I love showing students that learning grammar doesn’t have to be boring. I had a group of teenagers who dreaded grammar lessons, so I turned it into a competition with points and prizes. By the end, not only did they master the rules, but they also started to see grammar as a puzzle they enjoyed solving!"

Stories from students

Aya's Journey: From Beginner to Fluent

Aya, a 14-year-old from Japan, joined Khaway School with very basic English. Her dream was to study abroad in an English-speaking country. Over a year, Aya worked hard with her teacher, practicing speaking and writing daily. Today, Aya is preparing for her first TOEFL exam, and her confidence is sky-high.

Samir's Adventure: Learning English with Games

Samir, an 8-year-old from Egypt, struggled to stay focused during traditional lessons. His teacher incorporated his love for video games into the curriculum, using interactive games and activities to teach vocabulary and sentence structure. Now, Samir looks forward to every class and proudly shows off his new skills to his parents.
